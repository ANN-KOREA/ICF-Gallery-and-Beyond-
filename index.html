Chapter 1: Strolling Through the ICF Gallery: Viewing Health as 'Functioning'

In this chapter, we embark on a visual and philosophical journey through the ICF Gallery, reimagining health not as the mere absence of disease, but as the dynamic balance of functioning. Using evocative metaphors — the rainbow’s spectrum of abilities, the resonant bell of interconnectedness, the Yin and Yang of dualities, and the puzzle of human life — we explore how ICF visualizes life’s complexity. Readers will gain an intuitive grasp of how time, space, and the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) bring health assessment to life. The "ICF Gallery" itself, as envisioned by Dr. Kang, uses these symbols to underscore the idea that each individual is the protagonist of their own life, acting within a specific context (Time and Place) to optimize their functioning. The overarching goal is to see health holistically, integrating various data points (Big Data) into a harmonious understanding of well-being.

Chapter 2: IDENTITY: Redefining Health, Functioning (Salm), and Disability (Jilm)

What is health beyond the absence of illness? This chapter explores how ICF redefines identity itself through the interconnected concepts of health, functioning, and disability. We dissect the WHO’s biopsychosocial model, delve into the profound Korean concepts of "Salm" (Life/Functioning) and "Jilm" (Burden/Disability) as introduced in "ICF Gallery," and examine how work ability and rehabilitation form essential components of an integrated life. The reader will see how identity expands when framed through the lens of capabilities and aspirations, moving away from a purely medical definition of health to one that encompasses the entirety of human experience and interaction with the environment. This chapter also touches upon how various national systems are beginning to integrate these broader concepts into assessments of work capacity and social support, marking rehabilitation as a "third medicine" focused on quality of life.

Chapter 3: COMMUNICATION: The Language and Practical Application of ICF

ICF is not just a framework; it is a living language. This chapter provides a clear, actionable guide to understanding ICF’s structure, coding systems, and qualifiers. It traces the historical development from ICD and ICIDH to the comprehensive ICF model endorsed in 2001, emphasizing the shift to a person-centered, interactive paradigm. We explore the taxonomic hierarchy (Parts, Components, Domains, Categories, Levels) and the practicalities of assigning codes to body functions/structures, activities/participation, and environmental factors. Through real-world case studies, including rehabilitation cycles and interaction models, readers will learn to navigate the nuances of ICF, transforming it from theory into practice. This equips professionals and laypersons alike to communicate meaningfully about health and function across disciplines and cultures.

Chapter 4: FUTURE & CLASSIFICATION: Global Vision and Reference Standards

Here, we broaden our scope to understand ICF’s global impact and evolving future. We present the harmony between current global applications – from Germany's integration into medical education to Taiwan's Disability Rights Act revision – and the “Hot-Today” trends shaping the future of health classification. This chapter underscores ICF's role in fostering international collaboration and standardized data collection. A practical reference guide to the core ICF classification codes (summarizing the One-Level and Two-Level classifications for Body Functions, Body Structures, Activities & Participation, and Environmental Factors) helps the reader utilize these codes effectively, paving the way for global relevance and personal application. The vision is one of continuous evolution, where ICF adapts to new research and societal needs, promoting a truly universal understanding of functioning.
